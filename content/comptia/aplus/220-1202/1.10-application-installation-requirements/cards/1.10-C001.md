## Text

A 32-bit operating system is commonly limited to approximately
{{c1::4 GB}} of addressable memory.

## Extra

A 32-bit address space contains 2^32 possible values. Application and OS
architecture must still be checked separately from the amount of installed RAM.

## Instructor Notes

This fact explains a common 32-bit system limitation; it does not mean every
64-bit application will run merely because a computer has more than 4 GB of RAM.
