## Question

How does a dictionary password attack differ from a brute-force password
attack?

## Answer

A dictionary attack tries likely words and human-created patterns; brute force
systematically tries the possible password combinations.

## Instructor Notes

Dictionary attacks reduce the search space by exploiting predictable password
choices. Brute force is broader and may require much more time or computing
power.
