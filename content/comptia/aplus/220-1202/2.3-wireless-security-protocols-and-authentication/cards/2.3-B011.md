## Question

A sign-in requires a password followed by a code from a hardware token. Why is
this multifactor authentication, while requiring two passwords is not?

## Answer

The password and token come from different factor categories—something known
and something possessed. Two passwords are both knowledge factors.

## Instructor Notes

MFA counts distinct factor categories, not merely the number of prompts. It
adds authentication assurance but does not provide wireless encryption.
