## Question

Why do a password and a second security question not constitute true
multifactor authentication?

## Answer

Both are knowledge evidence; MFA requires evidence from different factor categories.

## Instructor Notes

A password plus a hardware token combines knowledge and possession. Counting
two items from one category does not provide the cross-category protection MFA requires.
