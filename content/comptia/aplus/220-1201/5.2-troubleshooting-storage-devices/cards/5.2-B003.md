## Question

A laptop hard drive makes a loud grinding noise and the user says files open
slowly. What should you avoid doing first?

## Hint

Noisy storage plus slow access raises the data-risk level.

## Answer

**Avoid running write-heavy repair attempts before protecting the data or
escalating for recovery.**

## Instructor Notes

### Reasoning

Grinding or clicking from a mechanical drive is a serious failure clue. The
interview-friendly answer is to stop treating it like a routine software issue,
preserve data if possible, and avoid actions that could make recovery harder.
