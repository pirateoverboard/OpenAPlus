# Objective 5.3 interview answer bank

These talk tracks support Objective 5.3 interview practice. Keep regular Anki
cards focused on one display troubleshooting decision.

## What to say first

- "I would first confirm whether the issue follows the display, cable, port,
  adapter, computer, or location."
- "I would check power, input source, brightness, and cable seating before
  replacing a monitor."
- "I would use known-good swaps to isolate the display path before choosing a
  fix."

## Information to gather

- Exact visible symptom: no signal, dim image, wrong color, flicker, artifacts,
  stretched image, or no audio.
- When it started and whether anything was moved, updated, docked, or replaced.
- Whether video appears before the operating system loads.
- Which cable, port, display, adapter, and settings have been tested.
- Whether the symptom affects one app, one display, or every output.

## Reasoning language

- "Video before the OS loads but not after points toward OS display settings or
  driver behavior."
- "Movement-sensitive flicker points first to the signal path."
- "A fixed black spot across computers points toward the panel, not the OS."
- "A projector has heat, bulb, filter, and cooldown variables that a monitor
  does not."

## What to document

- Exact symptom and when it started.
- Cable, port, display, adapter, and known-good test results.
- Display settings checked, including resolution, refresh rate, scaling, and
  brightness where relevant.
- Driver or OS changes.
- Projector airflow, filter, bulb, or heat details when applicable.

## When to escalate

- Known-good swaps isolate the issue to a failed panel, projector, adapter, or
  graphics path.
- A backlight or physical panel defect is suspected.
- Projector heat, bulb, or maintenance requires facilities or vendor service.
- The display problem remains unresolved after source-backed isolation steps.
