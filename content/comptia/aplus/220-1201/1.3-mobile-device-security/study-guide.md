## BYOD, COPE, and CYOD

Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) means the employee owns the device but must meet
company requirements. It can be difficult to secure because the same device may
hold personal and company data.

Corporate Owned, Personally Enabled (COPE) means the company buys and controls
the device while allowing personal use. Choose Your Own Device (CYOD) is similar
to COPE, but the user chooses from approved device options.

These ownership models matter because they affect control, user choice, privacy,
and how much authority the organization has over device settings and company
data.
