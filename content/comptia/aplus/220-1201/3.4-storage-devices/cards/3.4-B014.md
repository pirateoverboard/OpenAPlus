## Question

How does RAID 6 differ from RAID 5?

## Answer

RAID 6 adds a second parity set, requires at least four drives, and can keep data available after two drive failures.

## Instructor Notes

### Why

RAID 6 improves failure tolerance compared with RAID 5, but the extra parity consumes capacity and can add overhead.
