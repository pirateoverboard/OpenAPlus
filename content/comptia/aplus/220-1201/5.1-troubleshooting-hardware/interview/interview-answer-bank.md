# Objective 5.1 interview answer bank

These short talk tracks support Objective 5.1 interview practice. Keep regular
Anki cards focused on one retrieval target.

## What to say first

- "I would first gather the exact symptom, when it started, what changed, and
  whether it affects one app, one device, or the whole system."
- "I would check simple external causes before moving to invasive hardware
  work."
- "If there is a safety symptom like smoke or a burning smell, I would stop use
  and disconnect power before normal troubleshooting."

## Information to gather

- Exact error text, screenshots, or beep/code pattern.
- Recent driver, hardware, firmware, update, or application changes.
- Whether the issue happens before the OS loads, after login, at shutdown, or
  under load.
- Whether the problem is app-specific, user-specific, or device-wide.
- What tests have already been attempted and what changed.

## Reasoning language

- "A pre-OS symptom points me toward POST, firmware, power, or hardware before
  operating-system repair."
- "A symptom that appears under sustained load makes heat and power evidence
  important."
- "A single app crash is narrower than a full system crash, so I would check app
  logs and reliability history first."

## What to document

- Symptom, timing, frequency, and recent changes.
- Exact messages, screenshots, beep patterns, and logs.
- Diagnostics run and results.
- Physical checks performed, such as power path, cables, fans, and airflow.
- Current impact and next recommended action.

## When to escalate

- Safety symptoms such as smoke, burning smell, or visible damage.
- Intermittent crashes that remain unresolved after evidence-based checks.
- Hardware faults requiring replacement outside the technician's role.
- Symptoms involving business-critical work where continued testing increases
  risk.
