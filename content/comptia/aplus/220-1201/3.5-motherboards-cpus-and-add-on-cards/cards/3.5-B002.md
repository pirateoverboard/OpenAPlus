## Question

A desktop build needs a smaller motherboard but still needs broad case and power compatibility. Why might microATX be a reasonable choice?

## Answer

microATX is smaller than ATX but commonly keeps similar mounting and power compatibility, with the main tradeoff being fewer expansion slots.

## Instructor Notes

### Distinction

microATX is not the smallest option in the objective. It is a middle ground between full-size ATX expansion and very compact ITX systems.
