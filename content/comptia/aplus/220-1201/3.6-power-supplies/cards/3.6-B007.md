## Question

Why do many ATX power supplies use a 20+4-pin motherboard power connector?

## Answer

The extra four-pin section lets the connector support either older 20-pin motherboards or newer 24-pin motherboard power sockets when attached.

## Instructor Notes

### Distinction

The 20+4-pin connector is the main motherboard power connector. It is separate from auxiliary PCIe power connectors used by some high-power add-on cards.
