# Instructor Notes guide

Instructor Notes explain the value behind an answer. They must add information
that improves understanding, avoids a mistake, or connects the fact to practice.
Do not restate the answer in longer words.

Instructor Notes are required for accepted production cards unless there is a
documented reason they add no value. Record an omission in the objective
checklist or changelog. An unexplained validator warning blocks objective
completion.

Keep notes concise—normally a short paragraph or a few focused bullets. Use any
of these optional level-three headings when they add value:

- `### Why` — the reasoning that makes the answer correct.
- `### Exam Trap` — a plausible confusion and the condition that rules it out.
- `### Real Help Desk` — a safe, realistic operational implication.
- `### Memory Trick` — a short mnemonic that does not distort the fact.
- `### Related Cards` — stable IDs of cards that provide necessary contrast.
- `### Tech Wisdom` — a durable practical principle supported by the source.
- `### Source Note` — a concise clarification about source scope or wording.

### Good example

```markdown
## Instructor Notes

### Why

`[Action A]` comes first because the scenario has not yet established
`[prerequisite]`.

### Exam Trap

Do not choose `[Action B]` until the stated prerequisite is confirmed.
```

This adds decision logic without introducing another answer.

### Bad example

```markdown
## Instructor Notes

The answer is `[answer]`. Remember that `[answer]` is the answer.
```

This repeats the card and should be removed or replaced with useful reasoning.
Notes must not add unsupported facts, personal folklore, or unnecessary trivia.
